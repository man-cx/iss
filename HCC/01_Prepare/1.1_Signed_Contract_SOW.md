### 4. Acceptance

**4.1 Acceptance Process**
- ISS, on receipt of notification from SAP CLOUD Service Provider that the Deliverable is ready for acceptance, shall provide written notification within reasonable Business Days, with minimum impact to the agreed project timeline
- ISS shall notify SAP CLOUD Service Provider that the Deliverable has either been:
  - Accepted, OR
  - Rejected (failed to comply in all material respects with the applicable specifications in a Statement of Work)
**4.2 Rejection and Rectification**
- Where ISS rejects a Deliverable on the basis of material defects, SAP CLOUD Service Provider shall attempt to rectify such defects within a period commensurate with their severity
- After notification of defect rectification, Customer shall within **ten (10) Business Days** re-test the Deliverable and follow the procedure in Section 4.1
- If Deliverable still displays material deviations from agreed specifications after acceptance test, both parties will discuss to resolve the matter amicably
**4.3 Deemed Acceptance**
- If ISS does not serve notice of acceptance within the specified period and does not indicate rejection, Customer will be **deemed to have accepted** the Deliverable
- A Deliverable shall also be deemed accepted if ISS uses the Deliverable in live operation for a period in excess of **ten (10) days**
- If unable to meet inspection on time due to objective reasons, the deadline may be extended as appropriate by mutual agreement

### 7. Confidentiality

**7.1 Non-Disclosure Obligation**
- Neither party may disclose the other party's and the Customer's confidential information
- Cannot use it for any purpose other than the purpose for which the confidential information was obtained
- Use is permitted if necessary within the scope of the performance of the agreement and is agreed by the other party
**7.2 Exceptions**
Both parties will take all reasonable precautions to ensure they comply with their confidentiality obligations without limit in time. Restrictions do not apply if information or data:
1. Was already the legal property of the receiving party before it was obtained from the party in question
2. Was developed independently by the receiving party without using information or data of the party in question
3. Is or will be generally known or accessible other than by an act or omission on the part of the receiving party
4. Is disclosed to the receiving party by a third party without an obligation of confidentiality toward the party in question being infringed
**Legal Disclosure Exception:**
- Confidentiality obligations do not apply if Confidential Information must be made public pursuant to law, ordinance, court order or government agency decision
- Condition: receiving party makes every effort to limit scope of publication and notifies the party concerned in advance of any such intended publication
**7.3 Employee Obligations**
- Parties guarantee that their Employees and third parties engaged by the parties will comply with the confidentiality obligations
